How to play?:



* Goal: Get your 15 stones to the finish
* Player 1 starts on column 1 and the goal is past column 24
* Player 2 starts on column 24 and the goal is past column 1
* The stones are laid out on the board by themselves at the start of the game
* Player one starts, then player 2
* Each round two dice are thrown; after throwing, dice rolling is locked and the operation buttons become available
* For a player to move a stone, they must first select the column where their stones are, using a radio button
* Then they simply use the movement buttons
* When a player rolls 2 different numbers, the player gets 2 moves and can choose between 3 buttons (Move with die 1, Move with die 2, and Move with both dice); Move with die 1 and 2 each use up one move, while Move with both dice uses up both moves
* When a player rolls 2 matching numbers, they get 4 moves and the "Move for 2 matching dice" button becomes available, which costs 1 move
* A player can move a stone if the column is empty, the column contains their own stones, or it contains a single stone belonging to the other player (the opponent's stone is then knocked out and added to the knocked-out count)
* A player always has access to the "Restore stone" button, which can be used once per round; the same rules apply to it as to moving a stone (they cannot restore if the column is occupied by two or more of the other player's stones)
* The "Restore stone" button restores a stone from the knocked-out stones onto the player's starting column
* If a player cannot use all their moves, they can then use the "End round" button, which ends their round early
* When someone wins, a message appears with the option to save the results
* The game resets itself automatically at the end and can be started again
* A "Restart" button is available, which resets the game (it also includes a confirmation asking whether you really want to restart the game)
